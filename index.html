Mars - Our Neighbour

A Little Intro Of Our Friend

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second-smallest planet in the Solar System, larger only than Mercury. In the English language, Mars is named for the Roman god of war. Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere and has a crust primarily composed of elements similar to Earth's crust, as well as a core made of iron and nickel. Mars has surface features such as impact craters, valleys, dunes, and polar ice caps. Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons, Phobos and Deimos.Some of the most notable surface features on Mars include Olympus Mons, the largest volcano and highest-known mountain in the Solar System, and Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyons in the Solar System. The Borealis basin in the Northern Hemisphere covers approximately 40% of the planet and may be a large impact feature. Days and seasons on Mars are comparable to those of Earth, as the planets have a similar rotation period and tilt of the rotational axis relative to the ecliptic plane. Liquid water on the surface of Mars cannot exist due to low atmospheric pressure, which is less than 1% of the atmospheric pressure on Earth. Both of Mars's polar ice caps appear to be made largely of water. In the distant past, Mars was likely wetter, and thus possibly more suited for life. It is not known whether life has ever existed on Mars.

Earth Vs Mars Atmosphere

How Our Neighbour is Differnt From Us

Now, let us learn the differences between Earth and Mars in detail

Difference Between Earth and Mars
Earth Mars
The Earth's atmosphere primarily consists of nitrogen and oxygen. Mar's atmosphere primarily consists of carbon dioxide.
The length of the day on Earth is slightly under 24 hours. The length of the day on Mars is 24 hours, 37 minutes.
The time taken by the Earth to complete one orbit of the sun is 365 days. The time taken by Mars to complete one orbit of the sun is 687 Earth days.
The polar caps of the Earth are always covered with water ice. The polar caps of Mars are covered with a mixture of carbon dioxide ice and water ice.
The surface of the Earth is covered with 70% of liquid water. The surface of Mars is covered with bare rock and dust.
Earth’s atmosphere contains nitrogen, oxygen, carbon dioxide, water vapour and other  gases. Mars’ atmosphere contains carbon dioxide, argon, nitrogen and traces of oxygen and water.
Parameters such as geographical region, time of year, and elevation impact the temperature on the Earth which is 14 °C. Parameters such as distance from the sun, a thin atmosphere, tilted axis, and orbital eccentricity make the temperature of Mars vary.
The moon is the only natural satellite that orbits the Earth. There are two satellites that orbit Mars, and they are Phobos and Deimos.

Can We Live on Our Neighbouring Planet

Mars X Earth

Colonization or settlement of Mars is the theoretical human migration and long-term human establishment of Mars. The prospect has garnered interest from public space agencies and private corporations and has been extensively explored in science fiction writing, film, and art.Organizations have proposed plans for a human mission to Mars, the first step towards any colonization effort, but no person has set foot on the planet, and there have been no return missions. However, landers and rovers have successfully explored the planetary surface and delivered information about conditions on the ground. Mars' orbit is close to Earth's orbit and the asteroid belt. While Mars' day and general composition are similar to Earth, the planet is hostile to life. Mars has an unbreathable atmosphere, thin enough that its temperature on average fluctuates between −70 and 0 °C (−94 and 32 °F), yet thick enough to cause planet-wide dust storms. The barren landscape on Mars is covered by fine dust and intense ionizing radiation. Mars has in-situ resources, such as underground water, Martian soil, and ore, which could be leveraged by colonists. Opportunities to generate electricity via wind, solar, and nuclear power using resources on Mars are poor.
